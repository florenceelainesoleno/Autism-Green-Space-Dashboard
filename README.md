## 🌍 Project Story

**What if the environments where children grow up quietly shape patterns we rarely notice?**

Cities are more than roads and buildings—they are environments where childhood experiences unfold. Some communities grow within greener landscapes, while others face denser infrastructure, heavier traffic exposure, and reduced environmental buffering.

This project transforms environmental and public health data into an interactive dashboard that explores how urban conditions vary across California school districts and how these patterns may relate to childhood autism prevalence.

Rather than claiming direct causation, the goal is to reveal meaningful environmental patterns and encourage conversations around healthier, more sustainable, and data-informed communities.

---

## 📂 Dataset

This project is based on the study:

**Inverse Relationship Between Urban Green Space and Childhood Autism in California Elementary School Districts**
**Wu & Jackson (2017)**

### Included Indicators

* 🌳 Green Space Coverage
* 🛣 Road Density
* 🏙 Population Density
* 💰 Median Household Income
* 🧩 Autism Prevalence
* 🛡 Environmental Protection Metrics

The dataset was cleaned, transformed, standardized, and reduced to literature-supported variables for dashboard analysis.
